The Problem

AI personas are exploding.
None of them are accountable.

Museums are deploying AI visitor kiosks. Documentary producers are using AI for historical research. Educational publishers are building AI tutors modeled on real people. They all share the same failure mode:

"The model says something it shouldn't — and no one can explain why."

This creates reputational risk, legal exposure, and regulatory problems — especially as the EU AI Act takes effect in 2026. Yet no affordable, standardized tool exists to help institutions deploy AI responsibly for historical representation.

Current solutions are either unconstrained (fluent but unaccountable) or bespoke (€80,000–300,000 per deployment, non-interoperable, inaccessible to smaller institutions).

The Solution

A governance layer between the user and the persona.

Podobizna enables organizations to deploy AI representations of people that are explicitly constrained by evidence, role, and risk. The key architectural insight:

Users never interact with personas directly.
USER  →  MEDIATION LAYER  →  PERSONA ENGINE  →  RESPONSE
             │
         • Intent filtering
         • Source enforcement
         • Uncertainty injection
         • Intervention when needed
             │
       PERSONA CONTRACT
   (evidence · role · risk boundaries)

The Persona Contract is a formal specification — an open standard — that defines what sources a persona may draw from, what claims are permitted, how uncertainty must be expressed, and when the system must refuse rather than hallucinate. The model doesn't role-play. It operates under contract.

In Practice

Friction is the feature.

A museum visitor approaches a kiosk running a persona of Jan Masaryk, Czech diplomat and statesman:

Visitor
"Pan Masaryk, what did you think about the Munich Agreement?"
→ Mediation layer: legitimate historical question, sources available. Proceeds.
Masaryk (AI)
"It was a catastrophe. As I wrote to Lord Halifax in October 1938: 'We have been basely betrayed.'"
Source: MZV Archive, Document 1938-X-15 ↗
Visitor
"Would you support NATO today?"
→ Mediation layer: anachronistic question. Intercepts.
Mediator
"Masaryk died in 1948, before NATO existed. I can share what he wrote about collective security and alliances in his time. Would that help?"

Every response is grounded in verified sources. Every overreach is intercepted. Every refusal is explained. The system's accountability is structural — not a prompt engineering trick.

Who It's For

Two sectors. One infrastructure.

Museums

Interactive visitor experiences with historical figures — grounded in the archive, not the imagination. Scalable to any size institution.

Audiovisual

Documentary research, historical drama script consultation, character canon enforcement for production teams. AI that cites its sources.

Education

Historical figures in curriculum materials, university research tools, archival access interfaces. EU AI Act compliant by design.
