podobiznaA Czech word for depiction that emphasizes faithful likeness rather than an interpretation.

The problem

visitors spend seconds in front of artifacts.
how do we make an AI podobizna?

Existing AI tools cannot be trusted with historical personas. They hallucinate, invent, and embarrass institutions.

The insight

Institutions already have
the data.
They need the architecture.

Letters, inventories, diaries, photographs, 3D objects. The problem is not a lack of evidence. It is a lack of trust: that historical figures won't be misrepresented, and that digitised assets won't leave the building.

What Podobizna does

A visitor enters a room.
A historical persona
begins a conversation.

Grounded in the institution's own archive. Speaking only from documented evidence. Every claim traceable to a source. When it doesn't know, it says so.

For the institution: a new revenue stream, a reason to return, and a new life for collections that currently sit unnoticed.

How it works

The Corpus.
The Contract.

Corpus
Archival material is processed and indexed locally. The AI queries the Corpus. The original files never leave the building.
Contract
Every persona has a formal specification of what it can state, infer, and refuse. If a claim cannot be sourced, the persona says so. The specification is an open standard.

The experience

An investigation,
not a lecture.

Each space contains active personas — documented individuals or period-accurate figures — who speak only from evidence. Personas disagree, react to where the visitor has already been, and can withhold.

The interface is chosen by the institution: a personal phone, a mounted tablet, a projected voice, or a period object repurposed as a device. The conversation layer is the same.

Pilot — Prague, Czech Republic

Laichter House.
Liquidated in 1949
by the communists.

The space
Intact turn-of-the-century publishing house. The archive exists. A stress test: if Podobizna works here, it works anywhere.
The game
Should the house be liquidated? Jan Laichter, his son František, and communist minister Václav Kopecký. Five rooms. The archive is the evidence.

Why now

Three things
have converged.

Local AI is viable
Models capable of sophisticated dialogue now run on hardware a mid-size museum can afford. A year ago this was not true.
The EU AI Act
Institutions are acutely aware of liability when deploying AI that interacts with the public. Podobizna's local-first architecture is a direct answer.
Digitisation is complete
Most European institutions have completed major digitisation programs. The data exists. The problem is now what to do with it.

Who it is for

The beachhead:
cultural institutions.

The primary buyer is a museum's digital experience lead: responsible for visitor engagement and new revenue lines. Secondary decision-maker is legal or compliance. Podobizna's architecture is designed to satisfy their objections before they are raised.

From there: screen and documentary productions needing historically grounded personas. Local historians and heritage volunteers making undervisited sites legible to new audiences.

What it is not

Not a chatbot.
Not an audio guide.
Not a replacement.

It does not answer general questions about history. It does not deliver a fixed script. It does not replace curators or human guides.

It will not invent. It will not speculate. When it cannot answer, it says so.
