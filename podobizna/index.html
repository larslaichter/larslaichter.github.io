The problem

Museums are full of the most
compelling stories ever produced.
And visitors walk past them
in 90 seconds.

Why existing solutions fail

Audio guides inform.
AR entertains.
Neither makes the artifact
the destination.

Labels assume information transfer. AR adds a dragon to the castle and forgets the castle. Both approaches fail at the same thing: making the visitor want to look at what's actually there.

The insight

The most powerful thing
a site can offer isn't context.
It's voice.

What Podobizna does

Location-aware AI agents
at historical sites.

When a visitor enters a space or approaches an artifact, agents activate — grounded in that site, that period, those objects. They speak from evidence. They direct the visitor back to the original thing.

The agent is the reason to go there.
The artifact is the destination.

The cast

Any number of agents
per site.

Real personas
Historical individuals
Speak from documented sources — letters, inventories, diaries, trial records. Cannot speculate beyond evidence. Every claim traceable to a source.
Temporal simulations
Inhabitants of the period
A 13th-century Benedictine monk. A medieval guild craftsman. No individual identity — grounded in period-accurate knowledge, material culture, plausible social role.

The experience

A game.
The prizes are real
things in glass cases.

01 Enter a space. An agent activates.
02 Dialogue — constrained by evidence, period, role.
03 Agent reveals something the artifact contains.
04 Visitor examines the original.
05 Unlock — next agent, next space, next layer of story.

The game is not a quiz

"Find the inventory entry
that contradicts
what I just told you."

Agents disagree. Withhold. React to what the visitor has already seen. Issue challenges. The cast is a stateful cast — they know where you've been.

It's an investigation.

Pilot site — Prague, Czech Republic

Laichterův dům.
A publishing house
that changed Czech history.

The space
Intact turn-of-century house. Jan Laichter published Masaryk, Čapek, the avant-garde — 1895 to 1949. The original Boston Platen Press is still in the basement.
The game
Reconstruct the decision to publish Wolfgang Langhoff's concentration camp memoir in 1936 — the book that nearly destroyed the house.

Agents: Jan Laichter · František Laichter · Helena Malířová · a 1930s Prague typesetter
